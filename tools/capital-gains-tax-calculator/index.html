What Are Capital Gains?

A capital gain is the profit you make when you sell an asset for more than you paid for it. The IRS taxes these gains differently depending on how long you held the asset.

Short-Term Capital Gains (Held < 1 Year)

Taxed at your ordinary income tax rate — the same rate as your salary. This can be as high as 37% for high earners. If you buy a stock in March and sell it in December for a profit, that's a short-term gain.

Long-Term Capital Gains (Held ≥ 1 Year)

Taxed at preferential rates of 0%, 15%, or 20%, depending on your taxable income. This is the key tax advantage of holding investments for at least one year — the difference between 37% (short-term) and 15-20% (long-term) is enormous.

2025 Long-Term Capital Gains Tax Rates

Single Filers:

  • 0% — Taxable income up to $48,350
  • 15% — Taxable income $48,351 to $533,400
  • 20% — Taxable income over $533,400

Married Filing Jointly:

  • 0% — Taxable income up to $96,700
  • 15% — Taxable income $96,701 to $600,050
  • 20% — Taxable income over $600,050

Head of Household:

  • 0% — Taxable income up to $64,750
  • 15% — Taxable income $64,751 to $566,700
  • 20% — Taxable income over $566,700

Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

If your modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) exceeds certain thresholds, you'll pay an additional 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax on your investment income:

  • Single: MAGI over $200,000
  • Married Filing Jointly: MAGI over $250,000
  • Head of Household: MAGI over $200,000

The NIIT applies to the lesser of your net investment income or the amount by which your MAGI exceeds the threshold. This effectively makes the top long-term capital gains rate 23.8% (20% + 3.8%).

State Capital Gains Tax

Most states tax capital gains as ordinary income. A few states have no income tax at all:

  • No state tax: TX, FL, WA, NV, WY, AK, SD, NH (dividends only), TN (dividends only)
  • Highest rates: CA (13.3%), NY (10.9%), NJ (10.75%), HI (11%)
  • Moderate rates: IL (4.95%), PA (3.07%), MI (4.25%)

For someone with a $100,000 capital gain in California, state tax alone could be $13,300 on top of federal tax. This is why many high-income people establish residency in no-tax states before selling large positions.

How to Minimize Capital Gains Tax

  1. Hold for over 1 year — Convert short-term gains (up to 37%) to long-term gains (15-20%).
  2. Tax-loss harvesting — Sell losing investments to offset gains. Up to $3,000 in excess losses can offset ordinary income.
  3. Use tax-advantaged accounts — Gains in 401(k), IRA, and Roth IRA accounts are tax-deferred or tax-free.
  4. Donate appreciated assets — Give stocks to charity instead of cash. You deduct the fair market value and pay no capital gains tax.
  5. Step-up in basis — Heirs receive assets at the fair market value at the date of death, eliminating all unrealized gains.
  6. Qualified Small Business Stock — Section 1202 allows exclusion of up to $10M (or 10x basis) in gains from qualified small business stock held 5+ years.
  7. Primary residence exclusion — Exclude up to $250K (single) or $500K (married) in gains on your primary home if you lived there 2 of the last 5 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to pay capital gains tax if I don't sell?
No. Capital gains tax is only triggered when you sell an asset. Unrealized gains (paper gains) are not taxed. This is why buy-and-hold investing is tax-efficient — you defer taxes until you sell, allowing compounding to work uninterrupted.
How is crypto taxed?
The IRS treats cryptocurrency as property. Selling crypto for fiat, trading one crypto for another, and using crypto to buy goods/services all trigger capital gains tax. Holding crypto in a wallet without selling does not. See our crypto tax calculator for a dedicated tool.
Can I deduct capital losses?
Yes. Capital losses offset capital gains dollar for dollar. If your losses exceed your gains, you can deduct up to $3,000 ($1,500 if married filing separately) against ordinary income per year. Excess losses carry forward indefinitely to future years.
What is the wash sale rule?
If you sell a security at a loss and buy a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale, you cannot deduct the loss. The disallowed loss is added to the cost basis of the new purchase. This rule applies to stocks, bonds, and options, but not to crypto (as of 2025).
Does reinvesting dividends avoid capital gains tax?
No. Dividends are taxable in the year they're received, whether you reinvest them or take them as cash. However, reinvested dividends increase your cost basis, which reduces your capital gain when you eventually sell.
What is the capital gains tax on real estate?
For your primary residence, you can exclude up to $250K (single) or $500K (married) in gains if you lived there 2 of the last 5 years. For investment properties, gains are taxed at capital gains rates, and you may also owe depreciation recapture tax (25% on previously deducted depreciation). A 1031 exchange can defer capital gains tax on investment properties if you reinvest in a similar property.
