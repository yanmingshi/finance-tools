Salary to Hourly Calculator

Convert any salary to hourly, weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, and monthly rates. See your true pay rate and how overtime affects your earnings.

Salary to Hourly Conversion Formula

The basic formula depends on your work schedule:

Hourly Rate = Annual Salary ÷ (Hours per Week × Weeks per Year)
Hourly Rate = Annual Salary ÷ 2,080  (for 40 hrs/wk, 52 weeks)

Common Conversions

  • Annual to Monthly: Annual ÷ 12
  • Annual to Bi-weekly: Annual ÷ 26
  • Annual to Semi-monthly: Annual ÷ 24
  • Annual to Weekly: Annual ÷ 52
  • Hourly to Annual: Hourly × Hours/Week × Weeks/Year
  • Monthly to Hourly: (Monthly × 12) ÷ 2,080

Overtime Calculation

Under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), non-exempt employees must be paid 1.5x their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Some states have additional overtime rules:

  • California: 1.5x after 8 hours/day, 2x after 12 hours/day or after 8 hours on the 7th consecutive day
  • Alaska: 1.5x after 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week
  • Nevada: 1.5x after 8 hours/day (if earning less than 1.5x minimum wage)
  • Colorado: 1.5x after 12 hours/day or 40 hours/week

Salary vs Hourly: Which Is Better?

Advantages of Salary

  • Predictable income for budgeting
  • Often includes benefits (health insurance, PTO, retirement)
  • May include bonuses and profit-sharing
  • Exempt from overtime tracking (but also don't get overtime pay)

Advantages of Hourly

  • Overtime pay (1.5x) for extra hours
  • Clear compensation for actual time worked
  • More flexible scheduling
  • May get premium pay for holidays/weekends

The key question is: how many hours do you actually work? A $80,000 salary at 40 hours/week = $38.46/hour. But if you regularly work 50 hours/week, your effective hourly rate drops to $30.77 — equivalent to earning only $64,000 at 40 hours/week.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my true hourly rate from a salary?
Divide your annual salary by the total hours you actually work per year. If you earn $60,000 and work 40 hours/week for 52 weeks, your hourly rate is $60,000 ÷ 2,080 = $28.85. But if you actually work 50 hours/week, your real rate is $60,000 ÷ 2,600 = $23.08. Always factor in your actual hours, not just the "official" schedule.
What's the difference between bi-weekly and semi-monthly pay?
Bi-weekly means you get paid every two weeks (26 paychecks/year). Semi-monthly means you get paid twice per month (24 paychecks/year). Bi-weekly paychecks are slightly smaller but you get 2 extra paychecks per year. For example, $60,000/year: bi-weekly = $2,307.69/check, semi-monthly = $2,500/check.
Does this calculator account for taxes?
This calculator shows your gross (pre-tax) pay rates. For an estimate of your take-home pay after taxes, you'd need to factor in federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security (6.2%), and Medicare (1.45%). As a rough estimate, expect to take home about 70-80% of your gross pay, depending on your state and deductions.
How many working hours are in a year?
The standard calculation uses 2,080 hours (40 hours × 52 weeks). However, after accounting for 10 paid holidays and 15 days PTO, the actual working hours are closer to 1,920 per year. Some people use 1,950 hours (40 hrs × 52 weeks - 10 holidays - 5 PTO days) as a more realistic number.
What is a good hourly rate in 2025?
The median hourly wage in the U.S. is approximately $28-30/hour as of 2025. A "good" hourly rate depends on your location and field. In high-cost areas (SF, NYC), $40-60+/hour may be needed for a comfortable life. In lower-cost areas, $25-35/hour can be quite comfortable. Professional/specialized roles often command $50-150+/hour.
