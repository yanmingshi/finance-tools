What Is Cost of Living?

Cost of living measures how much money you need to maintain a certain standard of living in a specific location. It includes housing, food, transportation, healthcare, taxes, and other daily expenses.

The biggest factor is usually housing, which can vary by 300%+ between cities. A $2,000/month apartment in Manhattan might cost $800/month in Houston or $600/month in Memphis. This single difference can outweigh everything else combined.

Major Cost of Living Factors

  • Housing (30-50% of budget): Rent/mortgage, property tax, insurance
  • Transportation (10-15%): Car payment, gas, insurance, public transit
  • Food (10-15%): Groceries and dining out
  • Healthcare (5-10%): Insurance premiums, out-of-pocket costs
  • Taxes (variable): State income tax, property tax, sales tax
  • Utilities (5-8%): Electricity, water, internet

Don't Forget State Taxes

Cost of living comparisons often overlook income tax differences. Moving from a high-tax state to a no-tax state can be worth $10,000+ per year:

  • No state income tax: TX, FL, WA, NV, WY, AK, SD, NH (dividends only), TN (dividends only)
  • Highest state income tax: CA (13.3%), HI (11%), NY (10.9%), NJ (10.75%)

A $100,000 salary in California costs ~$9,300 in state tax alone. The same salary in Texas or Florida costs $0. That's a 9.3% effective "raise" just by moving.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this cost of living comparison?
Our calculator uses composite cost indices based on C2ER and government data. It's accurate for general comparison but individual circumstances vary greatly. Your actual costs depend on your lifestyle, family size, housing choice, and spending habits. Use this as a starting point and research specific costs for your situation.
Should I move to a lower cost of living area?
It depends on your salary adjustment. If you can keep the same salary (remote work), moving to a cheaper area is a significant raise. If you take a local job at local rates, the math may not work in your favor — salaries usually track cost of living. The sweet spot: keep a high-cost-area salary while living in a low-cost area (remote work).
How much salary do I need to maintain my lifestyle?
Use this calculator to find your salary equivalent. For example, if you earn $100K in San Francisco, you'd need about $55-65K in Houston to maintain the same standard of living — or you could keep your $100K salary and significantly upgrade your lifestyle.
Does cost of living affect salary negotiations?
Yes. Companies in high-cost areas typically offer higher salaries. When negotiating a remote position, companies may try to adjust based on your location. Know your market value and the cost differential so you can negotiate effectively. Some companies now offer location-independent pay regardless of where you live.
